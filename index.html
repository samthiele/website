The Earth is a complex, challenging planet, highly structured in some ways, highly unstructured in others, and largely hidden from view. As an earth scientist I enjoy the challenge of understanding, interpreting and inferring earth systems and processes.

I make geoscience digital, using data science and computer vision at their cutting edge to capture, integrate, synthesize and analyse diverse, multi-scale earth system data.

Things I'm interested in

Volcanology

I did my PhD on the interactions between dykes, stress and volcano instability. Volcanoes are fascinatingly variable and unpredictable, an important reminder that even baby geology is intricate and complex.

Structural Geology

One of the oldest geoscience disciplines, structural geology is key to understanding rock failure and fluid flow in the earth. This helps predict earthquakes and landslides, find ore deposits and, soon, capture and store carbon underground.

UAV Remote Sensing

Drones allow earth observation at unprecedented resolution and accuracy. But, extracting meaningful information from these datasets, and so building scientific understanding, remains a significant challenge.

3-D modelling and uncertainty

The Earth is 3-D, and mostly underground. This means the geometry of faults, ore-deposits and reservoirs, must be inferred from sparse observations, requiring sophisticated statistical and geometric methods and creating significant uncertainty.

Maybe I can help with

Exploration targeting

Geologically constrained but data-driven exploration requires a fusion of big-data machine learning with the minerals systems paradigm. But, naive approaches tend to fail; expert domain knowledge is still critical to ensure meaningful results.

Data visualisation

Visualising multi-dimensinal datasets is difficult but rewarding. I can use my graphics programming knowledge to design and build asthetic visualisations that identify and communicate otherwise subtle information.


GIS analysis and development

Mapping and GIS form an important part of all geosciences. I have extensive experience working with various GIS platforms, including the development of plugins for QGIS and ArcGIS.
